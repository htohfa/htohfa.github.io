PhD student in Astrophysics · University of Washington

I build computational tools to study how the universe went from a hot, featureless plasma to the galaxies and cosmic structures we observe today. Most of my work sits at the intersection of physics simulations, machine learning, and large astronomical datasets.

Right now I'm deploying the first neural network to control telescope optics in real-time for the Vera Rubin Observatory, developing emulators that replace months of supercomputer time with millisecond predictions, and training classifiers to find faint moving objects in the outer solar system.

Before UW, I studied physics and math at Bryn Mawr College and spent a summer at Microsoft Research teaching neural networks to identify mosquito species from wingbeat audio.

What I'm working on

Deployed on-sky

Real-time Neural Optics for the Vera Rubin Observatory

with Prof. Andrew Connolly · UW · 2023–present

Built and deployed a CNN that estimates wavefront aberrations from out-of-focus donut images, giving Rubin's Active Optics System 30× faster inference than the baseline solver. Trained on 256K synthetic images and transfer-learned on 2.1M real observations — now running during on-sky operations.

Active

Finding Faint Solar System Objects with Deep Learning

with Prof. Andrew Connolly · UW · 2026

Retrained the KBMOD moving-target classifier after discovering the original model was cheating via a normalization bug (AUC = 1.0 from epoch 1 — always a red flag). New ResNet with squeeze-and-excitation attention achieves AUC = 0.997 and recovers 3,318 novel candidates likely to be real TNOs.

Submitted

Emulating Cosmic Reionization with Neural Networks

with Prof. Matthew McQuinn · UW · 2024–2026

Deep-learning emulator for the ionizing photon mean free path, trained on 126 radiation-hydrodynamic simulations. Achieves 1.6% median error in milliseconds instead of hundreds of CPU-hours. Constrains the reionization midpoint to z = 6.8 ± 1.2.

arXiv: 2602.03923 →
In development

Neural Compression for Survey Astronomy

with Prof. Andrew Connolly · UW · 2024–present

Adaptive compression that learns which features in astronomical images matter scientifically, achieving 30× compression while maintaining 99% detection accuracy. Essential for Rubin's 20 TB/night data rate.

In development

PeriodicOrbit.jl

with Prof. Eric Agol & T. Chatchadanoraset · UW

Julia package that finds stable periodic orbits in resonant planetary systems using autodiff and Levenberg-Marquardt optimization. Enforcing periodicity breaks the mass-eccentricity degeneracy in transit timing data. Validated on HR 8799 (stable over 10,000 years).

Active

Baryon Streaming Velocity & Structure Formation

with Prof. Matthew McQuinn · UW · 2024–present

Implementing baryon streaming physics in MP-Gadget to study how the relative velocity between dark matter and baryons affects halo mass distributions and when the first structures form.
