Hurum Maksora Tohfa

PhD Student in Astrophysics • University of Washington

I'm working on some of the biggest questions in cosmology—from understanding how the universe's structure formed to testing whether fundamental constants have changed over cosmic time. Currently, I'm diving deep into how baryonic matter streaming affects structure formation and developing machine learning tools to extract more information from astronomical surveys.

Originally from Bangladesh, I've always been fascinated by mathematics and how it helps us understand the cosmos. My journey has taken me from studying fine-structure constant variations with CMB data to developing neural networks that can classify mosquito species by their wingbeats (yes, really!).

When I'm not debugging simulations or wrestling with datasets, I'm passionate about making physics accessible to everyone, especially students from underrepresented backgrounds.

Current Focus: Baryonic streaming velocity effects on structure formation
Latest Work: Real-time telescope optimization with deep learning
